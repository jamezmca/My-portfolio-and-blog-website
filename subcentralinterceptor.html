Central Interceptor

Wastewater Treatment System

I was a Project Engineer ensuring the successful delivery of Watercare’s $1.2bn Central Interceptor project in Auckland. I was a Watercare (the client) representative responsible for monitoring the Ghella-Abergheldie Joint Venture (GAJV) delivery of the Mangere Pump Station site. This role involved the understanding of key project objectives and quality auditing of the contractor deliverables. Seconded to Watercare, the reputation of both my company and the client was dependent on my performance.

I was responsible for fostering a strong relationship between the client and contractor, whose objectives on site can often conflict. Crystal clear communication was a minimum standard for maintaining a shared goal between all involved parties and being a constructive team player was key for successful and enjoyable project delivery. This role also taught me to look beyond the minimum scope of the project, be critical of the new and unfamiliar technical and health safety requirements of the job, and per the project slogan, go from “good to great”.
