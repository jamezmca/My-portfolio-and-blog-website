Calgary Drainage Tunnel

Segmental Lining and Shaft Design

On this project, I was the Design Engineer responsible for the design of a 6m diameter segmental lining for a 0.8km long water pipeline. This role required the interpretation of the provided geotechnical information and the corresponding design of the segmental lining, suitable for the variable geological material and profiles present along the alignment.

The extensive and complex analysis had to consider all possible failure mechanisms of the segmentally lined tunnel, including a high-level understanding of failure behavior to ensure the survival of the tunnel in the event of an ultimate limit state event â€“ such as an earthquake.

The design also included two shafts at either end of the tunnel, one to launch the TBM and another to receive the TBM. These shafts are designed to withstand earth pressures that press against the shaft walls.
