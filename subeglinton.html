Maintence Shaft Design

On this project, I was the Design Engineer responsible for the design of a maintenance shaft comprising of H-beam reinforced concrete 1m diameter piles. The maintenance shaft was to be approximately 20m x 20m with a base slab constructed 22m below ground level.

This design was especially complex as the bedrock was 30m below ground level and the soil above bedrock was very incompetent. The ultimate limit state loading actions on the shaft were very high but also the servicibility limit state deflections of the soil were high too. Designing a shaft system to withstand the design actions and minimise deformation of the structure required a very accurate modelling and analysis of the soil-structure interaction to result in a structural design that was conservative and safe but minimalistic simultaneously.

The construction process also had to be factored into the design as it influenced the deformation modelling of the system. A staged analysis had to be completed to account for the effect of a tiered excavation, as the structural loading and soild deformation varies at every construction stage.
